Ending Hidden Hunger in Rwanda

1. Introduction

Hidden hunger refers to micronutrient deficiencies that occur when people consume enough calories but lack essential vitamins and minerals such as vitamin A, iron, iodine, zinc, and folate. In Rwanda, hidden hunger is a silent crisis affecting millions of children and women...

3. Root Causes of Hidden Hunger in Rwanda

  • Heavy reliance on staple foods with low micronutrient content.
  • Poor dietary diversity.
  • Poverty and food insecurity.
  • Low awareness of nutrition.
  • Limited access to fortified foods.
  • Geographic inequalities.

4. Predicted Future if Hidden Hunger is Not Addressed

If no effective action is taken, Rwanda will continue to face high rates of child stunting with devastating long-term impacts...

5. Proposed Solutions to Eradicate Hidden Hunger

  • Food Fortification.
  • Bio fortification.
  • School Feeding Programs.
  • Nutrition Education.
  • Kitchen Gardens and Poultry Projects.
  • Strengthening Health Systems.

Ending hidden hunger in Rwanda is possible with coordinated efforts across all sectors...
